Weather at Different Latitudes

This website summarizes results of a project that leveraged a Python script to visualize weather of over 500 cities across the world. To get a snapshot of the maximum temperature, humidity, cloudiness, and wind speed of cities of varying distance from the equator, we leveraged a Python library called 'citipy' (https://pypi.python.org/pypi/citipy), as well as the OpenWeatherMap API (https://openweathermap.org/api).

As one would expect, the weather becomes significantly warmer as one approaches the equator (0 degrees latitude). The data also shows that the southern hemisphere tends to be warmer in January, when the dataset was generated, than the northern hemisphere. This may be due to the tilt of the earth. There appears to be no strong relationship between latitude and cloudiness, humidity, or wind speed.
